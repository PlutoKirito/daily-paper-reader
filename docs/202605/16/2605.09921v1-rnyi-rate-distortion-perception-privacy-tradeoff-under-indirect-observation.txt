I. I NTRODUCTION 

Modern compression systems must do far more than min-imise distortion. In semantic and goal-oriented communication for 6G and machine-to-machine networks [1]ÔÇô[3], the encoder is asked to preserve a latent quantity of interest S rather than the raw observation X. Recent studies on perception further show that reconstructions should be statistically realistic, i.e., they match a target marginal [4]. And with the development of generative models, users care more about their privacy and seek to reveal as little as possible about their personal information (e.g., a sensitive attribute U that is correlated with S [5]ÔÇô[7]). These three demands interact strongly, realism and faithful semantic recovery pull in opposite directions, and the operationally meaningful distortion-perception-privacy tradeoff should be quantified jointly. Most rateÔÇôdistortionÔÇôperception (RDP) work [4], [8]ÔÇô[10] treats the direct setting in which the encoder sees the very source it must reconstruct. Conventional communication sys-tem treats the setting where the encoder observes exactly the source that it must reconstruct. In semantic communication this assumption is rarely valid, and the latent target S (e.g. the spoken content of an audio, the underlying anatomy of a medical image, the state of a remote process) is considered.

The classical indirect or remote source coding problem [11]ÔÇô[13] captures exactly this asymmetry between the observation and the reconstruction target. Recent works have also extended it to other network problems and finite-blocklength analysis [14], [15] Blau and MichaeliÔÇÖs perceptual constraint dp(PX , P Y ) ÔëĄ

In the indirect setting this would be self-defeating, matching PX would make the reconstruction look like the noise-corrupted observation rather than the clean semantic source. The perceptual quality the user cares about is that Y Ôł╝ PS in distribution; e.g. the de-noised image must look like a clean medical image, not like a noisy scan. We therefore impose the perception constraint D╬▒(PS ÔłąPY ) ÔëĄ P ,which generalises [4] both to the indirect template and to the R┬┤ enyi family. Authors in [16] investigated this RDP problem under the indirect setting. The integration of privacy into this framework adds acritical layer of complexity. In information-theoretic privacy, standard mutual information has long been used to quantify average privacy leakage [6]. To capture worst-case privacy risks, the maximal leakage framework was introduced [7]. Bridging the gap between average and worst-case limits, recent literature has increasingly adopted R┬┤ enyi divergence and SibsonÔÇÖs ╬▒-mutual information as tunable, robust metrics for ╬▒-leakage [17], [18]. In the indirect semantic paradigm, a natural setup is that the noisy observation X contains a mixture of the semantic target S and the private attribute U

that has to be protected. Therefore, we generalize our recent formulation of the direct R┬┤ enyi Rate-Distortion-Perception (R-RDP) problem [19] by extending it to the indirect setting and provide a Gaussian characterization to illustrate the key tradeoff. On the achievability side, simulating the required stochastic test channel relies heavily on the Poisson functional repre-sentation, conceptually grounded in the Stochastic Functional Representation Lemma (SFRL) [20]. Especially, for order 

╬▒ > 1 R┬┤ enyi extension, the coding cost is investigated through a possibly loose logarithmic-moment bound because of the convexity of u╬▒ [19]. In this work, we improve it by characterizing the exact probability distribution of the Poisson selection index, and provide a sharper and more intrinsic

First, we formulate the indi-rect R-RDPP problem, replacing the standard direct perception constraint with a semantic realism metric and introducing a SibsonÔÇÖs mutual information criterion to track the private leakage. Second, we provide the exact optimal test channel for the scalar Gaussian regime, analytically proving the three-way tradeoff and an extension to a conditional criterion to isolate residual privacy leakage without penalizing semantic utility. Third, we refine the achievability bounds for the Poisson func-tional representation in the ╬▒ > 1 regime by establishing the exact geometric-mixture distribution of the Poisson index that improves upon existing logarithmic-moment approximations.
